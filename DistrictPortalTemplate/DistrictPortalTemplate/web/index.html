Tourism
Kamahi Devi Temple
The Kamahi Devi Temple is at a distance of around 11 km from Jhir di Khuhi on the main Mukerian-Talwara Road. It is situated in the Beh Nangal village and is believed to have been built by the Pandavas. This temple was originally named as the Kamakshi Devi Temple which was later on changed to Kamahi Devi Temple.
Citrus County, Hoshiarpur
Citrus County is a farmhouse in Hoshiarpur that spreads over 75 acres. Its premises encompass kinnow and other plantations along with flowers, palms and lush green lawns, besides having guest-houses.
Sanctuary Takhni-Rehmapur Wildlife Sanctuary
The Takhni-Rehmapur Wildlife Sanctuary lies on the foothills of the Shivalik Range of the Himalayas in the district of Hoshiarpur. The sanctuary, covered with chir pine, dry deciduous and moist deciduous forests, is home to several animals like barking deer, sambar, jungle cat, jackal, hare and wild boar, along with varied species of birds.
Gurdwara Guru Dian Tahalian
Gurdwara Guru Dian Tahalian located in Gaunspur village was built in remembrance of Guru Hargobind Sahib. It is believed the guru used to tie his horses on the tahli trees and hence the religious site has been named Gurdwara Guru Dian Tahalian.
